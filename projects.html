What is Gonorrhea?

Gonorrhea is the second most commonly reported disease in the US. It is found in both women and men. It is more common in young adults and teens and affects the genitals, rectum, and throat.It's an STD so it is spread through vaginal, anal, or oral sex, it can also infect infants through pregnant women.

How can you prevent Gonorrhea?

The most obvious and 100% way to prevent Gonorrhea is by practicing abstinence, but other ways can include practicing safe sex by using latex condoms and staying in a steady relationship with a partner that has been tested negative for Gonorrhea (and any other STD).

How do I know if I have Gonorrhea?

Men that have symptoms may experience:
A burning sensation when urinating; A white, yellow, or green discharge from the penis; Painful or swollen testicles (although this is less common).


Most women with gonorrhea do not have any symptoms. Women with gonorrhea are at risk of developing serious complications from the infection (like infertility), even if they don’t have any symptoms.
Symptoms in women can include:
Painful or burning sensation when urinating; Increased vaginal discharge; Vaginal bleeding between periods. Rectal infections may either cause no symptoms or cause symptoms in both men and women that may include: Discharge; Anal itching; Soreness; Bleeding; Painful bowel movements.


Can Gonorrhea be treated?

Yes, Gonorrhea can be treated using the right treatment. This includes: dual therapy, or using two drugs, to treat gonorrhea – a single dose of 250mg of intramuscular ceftriaxone AND 1g of oral azithromycin. It is important to take all of the medication prescribed to cure gonorrhea. However, Gonorrhea is becoming harder to treat do to the resistance the disease is taking on to the treatment.
